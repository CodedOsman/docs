## WIRING THE CIRCUIT

**Step 1:** Place the ultrasonic sensor and the three white LEDs on the breadboard. Ensure all components are firmly secured before making connections. Each LED must be connected with a 220Ω resistor to prevent damage.

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/Automatic_traffic_detention/circuit_1.png).

**Step 2:** Connect the VCC pin of the HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor to 5V on the Arduino Uno. Connect the GND pin to GND. Connect the Trig pin to Digital Pin 9 and the Echo pin to Digital Pin 10.

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/Automatic_traffic_detention/circuit_2.png).

**Step 3:** Connect the first white LED anode to Digital Pin 3 through a 220Ω resistor and its cathode to GND.
Connect the second white LED anode to Digital Pin 4 through a 220Ω resistor and its cathode to GND.
Connect the third white LED anode to Digital Pin 5 through a 220Ω resistor and its cathode to GND.


![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/Automatic_traffic_detention/circuit_3.png).

**Step 5:** After completing the wiring, connect the Arduino Uno to the computer using the USB cable.

## OBSERVATION

• The ultrasonic sensor continuously measures the distance of approaching vehicles or pedestrians.
• When an object is detected within 100 cm, all three white LEDs turn ON to simulate street lighting activation.
• When no object is detected, all LEDs remain OFF to save energy.
