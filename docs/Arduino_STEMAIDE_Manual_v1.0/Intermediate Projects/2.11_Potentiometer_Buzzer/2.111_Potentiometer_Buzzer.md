## WIRING THE CIRCUIT

**Step 1:** Connect one jumper wire from the 5V pin on the Arduino Uno to the BLUE RAIL of the breadboard and connect another jumper wire from the GND pin on the Arduino Uno to the RED RAIL of the breadboard.

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/potentiometer_and_buzzer/circuit_1.png).

**Step 2:** Place the ultrasonic sensor on the breadboard
Connect the ultrasonic sensor:
•	VCC → 5V 
•	GND → GND 
•	Trig → Pin 9 
•	Echo → Pin 10 

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/potentiometer_and_buzzer/circuit_2.png).

**Step 3:** Connect the buzzer:
•	Positive (+) → Pin 8 
•	Negative (-) → GND 

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/potentiometer_and_buzzer/circuit_3.png).

**Step 4:** Place the RGB LED on the breadboard
Connect the RGB LED:
•	Red → Pin 3 
•	Green → Pin 5 
•	Blue → Pin 6 

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/potentiometer_and_buzzer/circuit_4.png).

**Step 5:**
Place the potentiometer on the breadboard
Connect the potentiometer:
•	Left pin → 5V 
•	Right pin → GND 
•	Middle pin → A0 

![Mount the ultrasonic Sensor on the Breadboard](../../../assets/2.0/potentiometer_and_buzzer/circuit_5.png).

**Step 5:** After completing the wiring, connect the Arduino Uno to the computer using the USB cable.
