**Student & Teacher Manual**

**1. Project Overview**

Every commercial aircraft flying over Ghana is broadcasting its position, identity, altitude, and speed every second on 1090 MHz using ADS-B (Automatic Dependent Surveillance – Broadcast). With a simple RTL-SDR USB dongle, a laptop, and free open-source software, students can receive these signals and display every aircraft within 150 km on a live map — exactly what Air Traffic Controllers at Kotoka International Airport see on their radar screens. This project makes the invisible flow of air traffic over Ghana visible in real time.

**Learning Objectives**

* Define ADS-B and explain how it differs from primary and secondary radar surveillance
* Install dump1090 or FlightAware software and connect an RTL-SDR receiver to capture 1090 MHz ADS-B signals
* Identify at least 6 live aircraft from the display: callsign, altitude, speed, heading, and aircraft type
* Compare five surveillance technologies: primary radar, SSR, ADS-B Out, TCAS, and ADS-B In
* Explain why Ghana's GCAA is transitioning from radar-based surveillance to ADS-B as the primary surveillance method
* Present live surveillance data to the class in a structured 3-minute briefing

**5. Engineering Principles**

**Surveillance Technology Comparison**

| **System** | **Type** | **Range** | **How It Works** | **Aviation Use** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Radar** | Ground-based | 200+ nm | Transmits RF pulses; detects reflected signal from aircraft | ATC – detects all aircraft including non-cooperative |
| **Secondary Radar (SSR)** | Ground-based | 250 nm | Queries aircraft transponder; aircraft replies with code + altitude | ATC – adds identity and altitude to radar return |
| **ADS-B Out** | Airborne | 150+ nm (air-to-air) | Aircraft broadcasts GPS position, ID, altitude, speed via 1090 MHz | ATC + other aircraft; basis of modern surveillance |
| **TCAS (ACAS)** | Airborne | ±6 nm | Queries nearby transponders; issues climb/descend advisories | Collision avoidance on all commercial aircraft |
| **ADS-B In** | Airborne | 150 nm | Receives ADS-B Out from other aircraft; displays on cockpit screen | Situational awareness; traffic display |

|  |
| --- |
| **Why ADS-B is Revolutionising African Aviation**  Traditional radar requires expensive rotating antenna arrays, high-power transmitters, and specialist maintenance.  ADS-B ground stations cost 100× less to install and can provide identical coverage in flat terrain.  ICAO mandated ADS-B Out on all aircraft in most airspace classes by 2020.  Ghana's GCAA has been a leader in African ADS-B implementation — coverage now extends across much of West Africa.  The result: more aircraft can be tracked more accurately, with less infrastructure, improving safety across the region. |

**8. Common Errors & Fixes**

| **Error** | **Likely Cause** | **Fix** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **No aircraft detected** | Antenna not connected or poor location | Connect antenna; move to outdoor window; face antenna skyward |
| **Only a few aircraft** | Antenna range limited indoors | Use a longer coaxial cable to place the antenna at an outdoor window or rooftop |
| **Software won't install** | Wrong OS or missing drivers | Use the USB pre-configured Raspberry Pi image from Kit 11; plug-and-play |
| **Map shows wrong location** | Wrong coordinates entered | Set latitude/longitude to Accra: 5.6037° N, -0.1870° E in dump1090 config |
| **Aircraft positions jump** | GPS time offset in ADS-B stream | This is normal for some aircraft; position updates every 0.5–1 s |

**9. Upgrade & Extension Ideas**

* FlightAware Contribution: connect the Raspberry Pi to FlightAware.com; contribute live data to the global flight tracking network; receive a free FlightAware Enterprise account
* Traffic Pattern Analysis: log all aircraft for one hour; graph altitude vs. time; identify the Kotoka approach and departure corridors
* MLAT: with two or more RTL-SDR stations, implement multilateration to track non-ADS-B aircraft using signal timing
* Satellite ADS-B: research Aireon's satellite-based ADS-B system and how it provides global ocean coverage where ground stations cannot

* Support – Teacher-operated display; students fill in data table and complete the comparison table only
