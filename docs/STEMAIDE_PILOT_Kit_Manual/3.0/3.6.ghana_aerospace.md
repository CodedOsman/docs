**Student & Teacher Manual**

**1. Project Overview**

Aviation is one of Ghana's fastest-growing industries — and one of its least understood career pathways. This project gives students deep, personal knowledge of a specific aviation career track: from the academic and licensing requirements, through the training journey and first job, to mid-career progression and salary expectations. Every career is researched in the Ghanaian context, grounded in GCAA requirements, and illustrated with at least one real Ghanaian professional as a role model.

**Learning Objectives**

* Describe at least one aviation career track in detail: licensing pathway, training duration, minimum requirements, and entry-level role
* Identify and name at least one Ghanaian professional currently working in the chosen aviation career
* Explain the role of the Ghana Civil Aviation Authority (GCAA) in licensing aviation professionals
* Compare at least two aviation career tracks on a class-produced career map
* Deliver a structured 5-minute career profile presentation to the class
* Answer at least two questions from the audience about the chosen career

**3. Session Steps**

**Lesson 1 – Research & Poster Production**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 1** | **Career Assignment** |
|  | * Assign one career track to each group of 3–4 students (see Career Reference Table, Section 5) * Career options: Commercial Pilot, ATCO, Aircraft Maintenance Engineer, Avionics Technician, Airport Operations, UAV Operator, Aerospace Engineer * Groups research 5 specific questions about their career: * 1. What does this person do every day? * 2. What qualifications and licences are required in Ghana? * 3. Which training institutions in Ghana or nearby offer this pathway? * 4. What is the approximate starting salary in Ghana? * 5. Who is one Ghanaian professional currently doing this job — and what is their story? |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 2** | **Research Phase** |
|  | * Primary sources: GCAA official website (gcaa.com.gh), ICAO documentation, GACL website * Secondary sources: LinkedIn Ghana aviation professionals, Ghana Aviation News, Africa Aviation magazine * Each group must identify at least ONE named Ghanaian professional in their career track * Record all sources — no unsourced claims are accepted in the presentation * Teacher circulates and challenges vague facts: 'How do you know that? Can you show me the source?' |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 3** | **Poster Design** |
|  | * Poster must include: career title, daily duties, GCAA licensing pathway (as a flowchart), training time, salary range, and the Ghanaian professional profile * Include at least one diagram or image: e.g. a cockpit, an ATC display, a maintenance logbook, a drone * Use the Career Reference Table (Section 5) as a starting point — verify and expand all information |

**Lesson 2 – Presentation & Class Career Map**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 4** | **Group Presentations** |
|  | * Each group presents their career profile to the class (5 minutes maximum) * Structure: what this person does (1 min) → how to qualify in Ghana (2 min) → salary and job market (1 min) → the Ghanaian professional's story (1 min) * Each group member must speak for at least 60 seconds * Class asks two questions per group; group answers together |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 5** | **Class Career Map** |
|  | * After all presentations, the class produces a single A1 career map poster * Each career track is placed in a circle; connect with arrows showing potential career transitions * Add salary ranges, key qualifications, and the names of the Ghanaian professionals identified * Display the career map permanently in the Aviation classroom |

**4. Safety Notes**

|  |
| --- |
| **Research Integrity**  All facts must be sourced. The GCAA website, ICAO Annex documents, and official airline websites are the most reliable sources.  Do not use salary information from unverified sources — approach the GCAA or contact aviation companies directly for accurate local figures.  When identifying a Ghanaian professional, respect their privacy — only use publicly available information (LinkedIn public profiles, press releases, published interviews). |

**8. Common Errors & Fixes**

| **Error** | **Likely Cause** | **Fix** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Career facts incorrect** | Unverified internet sources | Cross-check all facts against GCAA official website (gcaa.com.gh) and ICAO documentation |
| **No Ghanaian professional found** | Research only looked at international examples | Search Ghana Aviation News, GCAA press releases, LinkedIn Ghana aviation community |
| **Presentation too long** | Poor time management during preparation | Practice with a stopwatch; allocate 45 seconds per slide maximum |
| **Salary data unavailable** | Ghanaian aviation salary data is not publicly listed | Use Africa Aviation salary surveys; contact GCAA HR department for guidance |
| **Career pathway unclear** | Multiple licensing options confused | Draw a simple flowchart: school → qualification → first job → progression |

**9. Upgrade & Extension Ideas**

* Guest Speaker: invite a Ghanaian pilot, ATCO, or AME to speak to the class — contact GCAA or AWA HR departments
* Airport Visit: arrange a guided visit to Kotoka International Airport operations area — contact the GACL Education Officer
* Salary Database: the class collectively creates an Aviation Careers Salary Database for Ghana; update it annually
* Career Pathway Video: record a 2-minute video interview with each group presenting their career; compile into a class documentary

**10. Teacher Notes & Differentiation**

**Lesson Planning Tips**

* Prepare a printed research pack for groups without internet access — print the GCAA licensing requirements pages and one airline career page per career track
* The Ghanaian professional requirement is the most powerful part of this project — dedicate time to it; students find it transformative to see someone who looks like them doing these jobs
* Contact the GCAA Public Affairs office before teaching this project — they sometimes provide guest speakers or informational materials for school programmes

**Differentiation Strategies**

* Support – Provide a structured research worksheet with the 5 questions pre-printed; focus on 3 career tracks only
* Core – Full research; poster; presentation; class career map
* Extension – Guest speaker coordination; airport visit; career documentary video
