**Student & Teacher Manual**

**1. Project Overview**

Aviation is the world's most regulated industry — and for good reason. The rules that govern every aspect of flight, from how a pilot earns their licence to how an airport paints its runway markings, are defined by ICAO and enforced by national authorities like Ghana's GCAA. This project gives students a thorough grounding in how aviation is regulated, who the key organisations are, and which ICAO Annexes are most relevant to the projects they have built and flown throughout this kit.

**Learning Objectives**

* Name and describe the six key aviation organisations: ICAO, GCAA, GACL, IATA, ECAC, and AFCAC
* Explain the difference between ICAO (international standard-setter) and GCAA (national regulator/enforcer)
* Identify at least 4 ICAO Annexes and explain the main requirement of each
* Research one specific GCAA regulatory requirement and explain how it affects aviation in Ghana
* Deliver a 4-minute group presentation on an assigned ICAO Annex, with at least one Ghana-specific example
* Score 80% or higher on a 10-question ICAO Annex identification quiz

**5. Organisation & Annex Reference**

**Key Aviation Organisations**

| **Organisation** | **Full Name** | **Role** | **Ghana Connection** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **ICAO** | International Civil Aviation Organisation | Sets global aviation standards (Annexes 1–19) | Ghana is an ICAO member state; GCAA implements ICAO standards |
| **GCAA** | Ghana Civil Aviation Authority | Regulates all aviation in Ghana; issues licences | Primary regulator; manages Kotoka and regional airports |
| **GACL** | Ghana Airports Company Limited | Owns and operates Ghana's airports | Operates Kotoka (DGAA), Kumasi (DGSI), Tamale (DGLE) and 7 others |
| **IATA** | International Air Transport Association | Trade body; sets commercial airline standards | AWA and Passion Air are IATA members |
| **ECAC** | European Civil Aviation Conference | Regional safety oversight | Ghana cooperates via ICAO regional office (WACAF) |
| **AFCAC** | African Civil Aviation Commission | African regulatory coordination | Ghana participates in AFCAC regulatory harmonisation |

**ICAO Annexes Most Relevant to This Kit**

| **Annex** | **Topic** | **Key Requirement** | **Relevance to This Kit** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | Personnel Licensing | Pilot, ATCO, AME licence requirements | Project 32 careers research |
| **2** | Rules of the Air | Navigation light colours; right-of-way rules | Project 29 navigation lights |
| **6** | Aircraft Operations | Flight and duty time; maintenance release | Project 44 maintenance log |
| **8** | Airworthiness | Aircraft certification standards | Projects 38 & 44 |
| **10** | Aeronautical Telecommunications | Radio frequencies and phraseology | Project 40 radio communication |
| **14** | Aerodromes | Runway markings; lighting; obstacle clearance | Project 32 airport operations career |
| **19** | Safety Management | SMS requirements for all aviation organisations | Safety culture in all projects |

**7. Common Errors & Fixes**

| **Error** | **Likely Cause** | **Fix** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **ICAO and GCAA confused** | Both described as making rules | Clarify: ICAO sets international standards; GCAA enforces them within Ghana — GCAA is the national implementation |
| **Annex content too vague** | Research from unreliable sources | Direct to ICAO.int for official Annex summaries; GCAA.com.gh for Ghana-specific regulations |
| **Presentation too long** | No time practice | Enforce 4-minute limit strictly; practice with a stopwatch in preparation |
| **Facts unverified** | Internet sources not cross-checked | Require all facts to be referenced; ICAO and GCAA official websites are the only accepted primary sources |
| **No Ghana examples** | Research only looked at global level | Ask: how does this rule affect a pilot flying from Accra to Kumasi? This focuses research locally |

**9. Upgrade & Extension Ideas**

* GCAA Visit: arrange a school visit to GCAA headquarters in Accra; meet a regulatory inspector; ask about current enforcement priorities
* Accident Case Study: research an aviation accident where a specific ICAO Annex requirement was violated; present the accident report findings
* Drone Regulations: research GCAA's specific drone regulations (Remote Pilot Certificate, notification requirements); create a compliance checklist for this kit's drone projects
* Safety Management System: design a simplified SMS (Safety Management System) for the school's aviation programme using ICAO Annex 19 as a guide

**10. Teacher Notes & Differentiation**

* Pre-download the GCAA.com.gh and ICAO.int pages on a school computer before Lesson 1 in case internet access is unreliable on the day
* The ICAO Annex quiz works best as a competitive team activity — teams earn points for each correct identification
* Support – Organisation matrix only; teacher provides completed Annex summaries; no independent research required
* Core – Full research; group presentation; class poster; Annex quiz
* Extension – GCAA visit; accident case study; drone regulations checklist; SMS design
