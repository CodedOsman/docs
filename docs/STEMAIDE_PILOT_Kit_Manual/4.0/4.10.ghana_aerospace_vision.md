**Student & Teacher Manual**

**1. Project Overview**

This is the final project of the Ghana Aviation and Aerospace Education Kit — and the most important. Everything built, flown, measured, programmed, and analysed across Projects 1–49 has been preparation for this moment. Student teams identify a real, unsolved aviation or aerospace challenge facing Ghana, develop an original technology solution, build or demonstrate it, and pitch it to a panel of judges in a format modelled on Ghana's most competitive innovation competitions. The best ideas from this project have the potential to become real ventures — contributing to Ghana's aviation future.

**Learning Objectives**

* Identify a specific, evidence-based aviation or aerospace challenge facing Ghana
* Develop an original aviation technology solution with quantified technical feasibility
* Reference at least 3 projects from this kit as technical evidence for the solution's viability
* Describe a realistic business model: who pays, how much, and how the solution is sustained
* Deliver a structured 7-minute innovation pitch to a panel, with every team member speaking
* Score 60% or higher on the five-criterion assessment rubric; respond to panel questions confidently

**3. Project Phases & Steps**

**Phase 1: Problem Definition (Lessons 1–3)**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 1** | **Problem Research & Quantification** |
|  | * Research your chosen Ghana aviation problem: find at least 2 statistics that prove it is real and significant * Examples: 'Ghana has X pilots per million people vs. Y in South Africa'; 'X% of cocoa farms have no disease monitoring'; 'NADMO received X flood damage reports in 2023 with no aerial assessment' * Write a 1-paragraph problem statement: what the problem is, who it affects, how many people, and what it costs * Teacher approves the problem statement before concept development begins |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 2** | **Stakeholder Mapping** |
|  | * Identify: who experiences this problem? Who would benefit from the solution? Who would pay for it? Who regulates it? * Map at least 3 stakeholders: e.g. Problem owner (NADMO), Customer (Government), Regulator (GCAA) * Contact one stakeholder if possible: write a brief email to GCAA, COCOBOD, or GHS explaining your project and asking one specific question about their need * Document any response received — it transforms the pitch from theoretical to real |

**Phase 2: Solution Development (Lessons 4–7)**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 3** | **Concept Generation & Selection** |
|  | * Apply the Project 45 requirements analysis: what must the solution do? What constraints exist? * Generate 3 solution concepts; evaluate with the Project 45 decision matrix * Select the highest-scoring concept; write a 1-paragraph justification * Every concept must: be technically feasible (reference kit projects as evidence), comply with GCAA regulations (reference Project 43), and address the defined Ghana problem |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 4** | **Build a Prototype or Demonstrator** |
|  | * Use the appropriate kit components to build a working prototype or functional demonstration * If the full solution is too complex, build the most critical subsystem: e.g. the payload release mechanism; the sensor system; the communication link * The prototype does not need to fly — it needs to demonstrate the key technical claim * Document the build with photographs; reference the specific kit projects that informed each design decision |

**Phase 3: Business Model & Pitch Prep (Lessons 8–11)**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 5** | **Business Model Development** |
|  | * Answer 5 questions: What do we sell? Who pays? How much? What does it cost to operate? How do we grow? * Express the cost in Ghana Cedis: 'Our drone service will cost GH₵ X per flight; customers pay GH₵ Y; profit per flight = GH₵ Z' * Identify a funding pathway: GCAA innovation grant, NGO partnership, government contract, or commercial subscription * Calculate: how many flights per day needed to cover operating costs? Is this realistic? |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 6** | **Pitch Deck Preparation** |
|  | * Create a visual pitch deck: maximum 8 slides or a poster board — no more * Follow the pitch structure in Section 5: Problem → Solution → Technical Feasibility → Ghana Context → Business Model → Ask * Every team member must rehearse their section at least 3 times * Practice answering the hardest questions: 'Why won't this fail? How much will it cost? What happens if GCAA says no?' |

**Phase 4: Pitch Day (Lessons 12–15)**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 7** | **Pitch Day Presentation** |
|  | * Each team presents to a panel: teacher + invited guests (GCAA official, aviation professional, entrepreneur, parent — invite at least one external judge) * 7 minutes presentation; 3 minutes questions * Panel scores using the rubric in Section 6 — scores are announced after all teams have presented * Award categories: Best Overall Pitch; Most Technically Grounded; Most Commercially Viable; Best Ghana Impact; Most Creative |

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **STEP 8** | **Post-Pitch Action** |
|  | * Every team writes one action they will take to advance their idea after this class: email a stakeholder, file a GCAA enquiry, apply for a school innovation grant, or present at a community event * Teacher collects these action commitments and follows up at the end of term * The best idea(s) are nominated for national competitions: Ghana Science & Maths Quiz, GCTU Innovation Challenge, or KNUST Entrepreneurship Award |

**5. Pitch Structure Guide**

| **Section** | **Duration** | **Content** | **Who Speaks** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1. The Problem** | 90 s | What Ghana aviation/aerospace challenge are you solving? Use one compelling statistic | All members introduce themselves; lead speaker opens |
| **2. Your Solution** | 2 min | What is your aviation technology idea? How does it work? Show a sketch or model | Lead speaker + technical lead |
| **3. Technical Feasibility** | 2 min | Why can it work? Reference projects from this kit; cite specific technical evidence | Technical lead |
| **4. Ghana Context** | 1 min | Why Ghana specifically? Who benefits? Connect to local organisations (GCAA, NADMO, cocoa industry) | Any member |
| **5. Business Model** | 1 min | How would this be funded or operated? Who is the customer? What is the cost? | Any member |
| **6. Ask & Closing** | 30 s | What do you need to make this happen? Thank the panel | Lead speaker |

**6. Assessment Rubric**

| **Criterion** | **Weight** | **Excellent (4)** | **Good (3)** | **Developing (2)** | **Beginning (1)** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Problem Clarity** | 15% | Specific, well-researched Ghanaian problem with data | Clear problem; some evidence | Vague problem | No clear problem identified |
| **Technical Soundness** | 25% | Feasible; references projects from this kit; quantified | Mostly feasible; limited reference | Partially feasible | Not technically grounded |
| **Ghana Relevance** | 20% | Deep local connection; named stakeholders; real need | Good local context | Some local connection | Generic; no Ghana specificity |
| **Innovation** | 20% | Novel approach; not previously implemented in Ghana | Creative but similar to existing solutions | Adaptation of known solution | Direct copy |
| **Presentation Quality** | 20% | Confident; all members speak; questions answered | Good delivery; most questions answered | Acceptable delivery | Unclear or incomplete |

**8. Common Errors & Fixes**

| **Error** | **Likely Cause** | **Fix** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Problem too vague** | 'Aviation in Ghana needs improvement' is not a problem | Quantify: 'Ghana has only 210 licensed pilots for 9 airlines — there is a shortage of X pilots per year' |
| **Solution not technically grounded** | No reference to what students have built | Every claim must reference a specific project: 'We know the payload can be carried because in Project 17...' |
| **No Ghana specificity** | Solution could apply anywhere | Name specific: locations, organisations, communities, regulatory bodies, and cost in Ghanaian Cedis |
| **Business model missing** | Students forgot this section | Every aviation service needs money to operate: government grant, airline contract, NGO partnership, or user fee |
| **Questions cannot be answered** | Preparation shallow | Anticipate the 3 hardest questions: cost, regulation, and what happens when it fails — prepare answers for these specifically |

**9. Going Further – After the Pitch**

* National Competition: submit the pitch to the Ghana Science & Maths Quiz aerospace category, GCTU Innovation Challenge, or KNUST Ashesi Hackathon
* GCAA Engagement: request a meeting with GCAA's Innovation and Digital Transformation team; present your idea to a real regulator
* Business Plan: extend the business model into a full 5-page business plan; apply for a YEA (Youth Enterprise Agency) grant
* Community Presentation: present the best pitch at a community event, Parent-Teacher Association meeting, or local government assembly
* Mentor Connection: reach out to Ghanaian aerospace professionals on LinkedIn; share your pitch deck and request 15 minutes of feedback

**10. Teacher Notes & Differentiation**

**Lesson Planning Tips**

* Invite at least one external judge to Pitch Day — a GCAA official, aviation company representative, or innovation organisation member transforms the event from a class exercise into a real opportunity
* The stakeholder contact step (Step 2) is the most transformative — even a single reply from GCAA or COCOBOD makes the project feel real to students
* Display all pitch decks in the classroom for the week after Pitch Day — celebrate the work publicly
* For the best idea(s), help students write a formal application to a national competition — this is where classroom projects become real ventures

**Differentiation Strategies**

* Support – Problem and solution provided by teacher; team focuses on demonstrating technical feasibility and presenting one section of the pitch
* Core – Full team-led process; all 6 pitch sections; prototype demonstration; panel questions answered
* Extension – Stakeholder contact; national competition submission; business plan; community presentation

**Assessment Summary**

* Technical Soundness (25%): This is the highest-weighted criterion — push students to reference specific projects, calculations, and data from the kit
* Ghana Relevance (20%): Named stakeholders, specific locations, and cost in Ghana Cedis are required for full marks
* Pass mark: 60% (30/50 weighted points). Distinction: 80%+ (40/50 weighted points)
